# Short-Link Neighbourhood Diagnostic

Created at: `2026-05-08T22:53:35+00:00`.

## Purpose

This diagnostic checks whether very short top-1% links are unusually high relative to their immediate graph neighbourhood. It does not change rankings, model scores, or published outputs.

## Method

Neighbours are defined from OpenRoads topology only: two links are neighbours when they share a `start_node` or `end_node` value in `data/processed/shapefiles/openroads.parquet`. Geometry is not used.

The top-1% file supplies the target links and their displayed fields. Because a top-1% link can have neighbours outside the top-1% output, `data/models/risk_scores_eb.parquet` is used only to attach `predicted_eb` to all graph neighbours before computing neighbourhood means and standard deviations.

For each top-1% link, the diagnostic computes `n_neighbours`, `n_scored_neighbours`, `neighbour_mean_predicted_eb`, `neighbour_sd_predicted_eb`, and `eb_z_vs_neighbours = (predicted_eb - neighbour_mean_predicted_eb) / neighbour_sd_predicted_eb`. The z-score is left blank when no positive neighbour standard deviation is available.

## Caveats

This is a graph-neighbour check, not a geometry-neighbour or corridor-level check. It can miss nearby parallel or crossing links that do not share an OpenRoads node, and it can compare links with different road contexts when they meet at complex junctions. The result should be used as a QA triage signal for short-link review, not as a reason to alter the ranking by itself.
